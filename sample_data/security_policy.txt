ACME Corp — Information Security & Acceptable Use Policy (Sample Document)
Version 3.1 | Effective March 2026

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 1 — PURPOSE AND SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This policy governs the use of ACME Corp's information systems, networks, devices, and data.
It applies to all full-time employees, contractors, and vendors who access ACME systems.
Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal proceedings.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 2 — DEVICE AND NETWORK SECURITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Approved Devices
Employees must use ACME-issued devices for all work involving company data or systems.
Personal devices (BYOD) are not permitted to access internal systems or store company data without prior IT approval and enrollment in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) program.

2.2 VPN Requirement
All remote access to internal ACME systems must use the company-approved VPN (currently Cloudflare WARP for Teams).
VPN is mandatory when working from any network outside ACME office premises, including home networks.
Working from public Wi-Fi (coffee shops, airports, hotels, co-working spaces) requires VPN to be active at all times.
Splitting VPN tunnels to bypass security controls is prohibited.

2.3 Password and Authentication
All accounts must use strong passwords: minimum 16 characters, mix of upper, lower, numbers, and symbols.
Passwords must not be reused across systems or shared with any other person, including managers or IT support.
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all ACME systems that support it.
Password managers are encouraged; ACME provides a company license to 1Password.

2.4 Software and Updates
Operating system and security patches must be applied within 7 days of release.
Only IT-approved software may be installed on ACME devices.
Employees must not disable antivirus, firewall, or endpoint detection software.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 3 — DATA HANDLING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Data Classification
Confidential: Customer data, financial records, source code, employee PII. Requires encryption at rest and in transit. Never share externally without legal approval.
Internal: Internal communications, project plans, meeting notes. Do not share publicly.
Public: Marketing materials, published blog posts. No restrictions.

3.2 Storage and Transmission
Confidential data must be stored in ACME-approved systems (Google Drive, Notion, or approved cloud storage).
Sending confidential data via personal email or unapproved file-sharing services is prohibited.
All confidential data transmitted externally must be encrypted.

3.3 Customer Data
Customer data must only be accessed for legitimate business purposes.
Customer data must never be used to train third-party AI models without explicit written consent from the customer and legal approval from ACME.
Any suspected customer data breach must be reported to the security team within 1 hour of discovery.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SECTION 4 — ACCEPTABLE USE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Permitted Use
ACME systems and networks are provided for business use. Incidental personal use is acceptable provided it does not violate this policy, consume significant resources, or interfere with work duties.

4.2 Prohibited Activities
The following are strictly prohibited on ACME systems or networks:
- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content of any kind
- Attempting to bypass, circumvent, or probe security controls
- Installing unauthorized software, extensions, or browser plugins
- Using ACME systems for personal commercial activities or cryptocurrency mining
- Sharing login credentials or allowing others to use your ACME accounts
- Accessing colleagues' files, emails, or systems without explicit authorization
- Using AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.) to process confidential or customer data without IT approval

4.3 AI Tool Usage
Employees may use approved AI productivity tools for drafting, summarizing, and coding assistance.
Approved tools: GitHub Copilot (engineering), Gemini for Workspace (all employees).
Unapproved AI tools must not be used to process any confidential, internal, or customer data.
Submitting customer data, source code, or proprietary business information to unapproved AI services is a serious security violation.

SECTION 5 — INCIDENT REPORTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All suspected security incidents — lost devices, phishing emails, unauthorized access, suspicious software — must be reported immediately to security@acmecorp.example.com or via the #security-incidents Slack channel.
Do not attempt to investigate or remediate incidents yourself.
Early reporting is always the right call; there is no penalty for good-faith reporting.
